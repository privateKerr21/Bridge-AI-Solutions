"The AI hallucinated."

"It wasn't smart enough."

"It kept getting edge cases wrong."

This is the comment section under every failed-AI-build post on Reddit.

Read past the headline and the reason almost always lives upstream of the model.

Three patterns:

**Messy data.** The AI inherits it.

**A process that only existed in someone's head.** The 20% nobody wrote down is where it breaks.

**No definition of "working."** Without one, every imperfect output becomes evidence the project failed.

The technology is mature enough. The bottleneck is the inputs.

Clean the data. Document the process. Define the outcome. Then build.

Where do most SMBs get tripped up — data, process, or expectations?
