If you run a digital marketing agency, you already know this one.

Client reporting used to be a few hours on a Friday. Now it's a full workweek a month for every account manager you have.

Here's where the hours actually go for a manager with eight clients:

→ 45 minutes pulling exports across five platforms
→ 1 hour consolidating the data into a single sheet
→ 45 minutes writing the executive summary in the client's voice
→ 1.5 hours formatting it into whatever the client wants this month — slides, doc, dashboard, Loom walkthrough

Four hours per client. Times eight clients. A full week, every month, before any strategic work happens.

And here's the part most owners don't see: the reporting tools you're already paying for — Whatagraph, AgencyAnalytics, Looker Studio — automate the easy 50%. The data pull. The dashboard. The chart that refreshes on the first of the month.

The other 50% is still sitting on your account manager's desk:

→ The narrative that explains what changed and why
→ The custom format every client wants
→ The exceptions that need context the dashboard can't see

That's where the workweek actually goes.

In reality, that half is automatable too — but not by another reporting platform. It's a thin custom layer that drafts the narrative, adapts the format, and surfaces the exceptions, on top of the dashboard you already have. Human edits the draft. Account manager still sends the report. The four hours becomes 45 minutes, and the 45 minutes is the part that actually requires judgment.

Agency owners — how many hours a month is reporting really taking at your shop when you add it all up?
