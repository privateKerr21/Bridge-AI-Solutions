Before you spend $10,000 on AI implementation, answer these 12 questions.

I've been digging into why AI projects fail.

The pattern is pretty consistent. The AI is almost never the problem.

Data scattered across five tools. A process that only existed in someone's head. A team member who found out about the new system when it went live.

So I put together a checklist. 12 questions, 4 categories: data, process, team, expectations.

Most people will get mixed answers. That's not a red flag — that's sequencing. Fix the gaps, then build.

Skip this and you're rolling the dice. The "$50k on AI, back on spreadsheets eight months later" posts on Reddit are almost always a readiness story, not a technology one.
