# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Owners and operators of small B2B service businesses in the United States, typically 1–20 people. They have real revenue and real operational pain — repetitive tasks eating hours every week, processes living in spreadsheets, work that depends on one person's memory. They are not technical, but they are sophisticated buyers: they have hired contractors before, been burned by both Zapier-style duct tape and SaaS that "almost fits," and they are evaluating Bridge alongside hiring an in-house developer or a freelancer.

When they land on the site, they are skeptical. They have seen the AI hype cycle. The job they are trying to get done is: decide in under two minutes whether Bridge is a serious partner worth a 30-minute discovery call, or whether to close the tab.

## Product Purpose

Bridge AI Solutions is a boutique consultancy that builds custom AI-powered software and tools for small B2B service businesses. The model: identify the single biggest operational bottleneck, build a real piece of software around it, hand it off so the client owns the code outright. Pricing is fixed per project (Focused Build $2,500, Signature Build $6,000–$8,000, Studio Partner $2,500/mo retainer).

The marketing site exists to do one thing: convert a skeptical, busy small-business owner into a discovery call booking. Success is one front door, taken seriously, with no friction between intent and calendar.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, opinionated, candid. Bridge has a point of view and is willing to say things other consultancies will not — about Zapier's limits, about SaaS that almost fits, about why owning the code matters. The voice is the founder's voice: confident, direct, no hedging, no jargon, no salesy throat-clearing. Editorial bite, not pitch-deck cadence.

Emotional goal: when a visitor leaves, they should feel they have just read something written by a competent professional who respects their time, not a marketing funnel.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI / SaaS landing pages**: gradient mesh backgrounds, dark glows, hero-metric templates, "Powered by AI" badges, glassmorphism, 3D blobs, neon-on-black, every-Vercel-template aesthetic.
- **Discount and urgency marketing**: strikethrough original prices, pulsing "limited spots" dots, countdown timers, "founding rate" theater, scarcity copy. This contradicts premium positioning and signals desperation.
- **Saturated anti-SaaS reflex lane**: cream + gold + Space Grotesk + 2px brutalist borders + hover-translate-shadow has itself become the default LLM reflex for "premium consultancy that isn't SaaS." Bridge should not be guessable from the category. The current site sits in this lane and needs to move out of it.
- **Agency cliché grids**: bento layouts, identical icon-card sections, scrolling logo bars, "We help teams ship faster" copy.

## Design Principles

1. **Quiet authority over loud confidence.** Restraint signals premium. Generous whitespace, tight typography, color used as accent not as identity. If the page were a person, it would be the one in the room speaking last.
2. **Show real software.** Bridge sells "real software you own" — the site must show real software. Screenshots, Looms, before/after artifacts. Abstract argument is not proof.
3. **One front door.** Every page leads to a single, defensible conversion path (Calendly). Multiple CTAs with multiple labels signal an unsettled sales process. Pick one and commit.
4. **Editorial discipline, not template repetition.** Long pages should read like a magazine feature with rhythm and visual rest, not like eight stacked label-h2-card sections. If a section does not earn its place, kill it.
5. **Heritage materiality, modern engineering.** Visual lane sits between a private bank's restraint and a heritage atelier's materiality (formal type, gilt accents used sparingly, weight in the page). The engineering register stays modern — nothing nostalgic, nothing skeuomorphic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. Specific care points:
- Color contrast: gold (#D4AF37) is decorative-only on light backgrounds; never used for body copy. Body type stays at ink (#0D0D0D) on bg (#F5F2EE) for AAA-grade contrast.
- Reduced motion: any motion (scroll-driven, hover translate, animated grids) must respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`.
- Keyboard navigation: every interactive element (Quiz, mobile nav, pricing CTAs) reachable and operable without a mouse, with visible focus states.
- No essential content delivered through color alone.
