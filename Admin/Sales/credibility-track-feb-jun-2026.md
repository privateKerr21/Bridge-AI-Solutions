# Bridge AI Solutions — Credibility Track
## February 4 – June 30, 2026
## Target Niche: Small CPA Firms (1–20 Professionals)

---

## Why This Track Exists

Credentials are table stakes. The firms winning in boutique AI consulting in 2026 are the ones who can say "we specifically serve [X type of firm]" and back it up with proof. This track does two things in parallel:

1. **Builds the credential stack** — fast, cheap, verifiable badges that pass the initial credibility filter
2. **Builds the proof assets** — case study, content, and positioning materials that actually close deals

The CPA niche is the target because the referral loop between wealth managers and CPAs is one of the tightest in professional services. Refined Wealth clients almost certainly have a CPA. That's the warm intro.

## February — Credential Foundations

### Week 1–2 (Feb 4–17)

**Make Academy — All 3 Levels**
- Cost: Free
- Time: ~15 hours, self-paced
- URL: academy.make.com
- Why: Make is the primary automation platform for the SMB workflow automations you're building. LinkedIn badges. Proof you completed official training, not just YouTube tutorials.
- Action: Complete Foundation → Basics → Advanced in sequence.

**Zapier Solution Partner — Apply**
- Cost: Free
- Time: ~1 hour to apply
- URL: zapier.com/l/solution-partner
- Why: This is the most undervalued item on this track. It's not just a badge — it's a partner program. Gets you into Zapier's directory, which is how non-technical business owners (like CPA firm principals) actually search for automation help. Commission revenue potential. Apply now, badges come as you deliver work.
- Action: Apply. Have a business website with a custom domain email ready.

### Week 3–4 (Feb 18–Mar 3)

**Google Cloud Generative AI Leader**
- Cost: $99 (exam only; prep is free)
- Time: ~10 hours study + 60-min exam
- URL: cloud.google.com/learn/certification/generative-ai-leader
- Why: The only major vendor cert designed for the advisor role, not the engineer role. Tests what your clients actually need you to know: what AI can realistically do, what the risks are, how to evaluate it. The responsible AI and governance content maps directly to the compliance concerns CPAs have.
- Action: Use Google Cloud Skills Boost for free prep. Schedule and sit the exam by end of February.

## March — Deepen + Begin the Long Game

### Week 1–2 (Mar 3–17)

**Microsoft AI Business Professional (AB-730)**
- Cost: ~$165
- Time: ~10 hours study + 45-min exam
- URL: learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/ai-business-professional/
- Why: Most small CPA firms run on Microsoft 365. This cert says "I understand AI in the tools you already use." Newly available (Feb 2026). The signal is: you understand their world, not just yours.

- Cost: Free
- Time: ~3 hours
- URL: docs.n8n.io/courses/
- Why: Growing fast among AI-native consultants. Low client recognition today, but relevant for compliance-sensitive firms that care about data sovereignty — which CPAs do. Knock it out while you're in certification mode.

This is the month that matters most. Everything in Feb–March gets you past the initial filter. April is what turns interest into a conversation.

You have the work. You have the pivot story, the real client pain point (Matt's quote about follow-ups falling through the cracks), the technical stack (Make, Zapier, Google Sheets), the two-track system design, the 8–10 hrs/week time savings target, and the compliance angle. What you don't have is a clean, numbers-driven writeup.

The case study is already drafted and saved at: `Assets/Credibility/case-study-refined-wealth.md`

**How to use it:**
- It's written with CPA translation in mind. The pain points (manual tracking, things falling through cracks, clunky data entry, compliance requirements) are the same pain points CPAs have.
- When you talk to a CPA prospect, you don't say "we did this for a wealth manager." You say "we solved this exact problem for a financial advisory firm — and it's the same problem we see in every CPA firm we talk to."
- The metrics in the case study are targets from the proposal (8–10 hrs/week, 80% data entry reduction). Once the Refined Wealth engagement is live and producing real numbers, update this file with actuals. Real numbers are infinitely more powerful than projections.

### Week 3–4 (Apr 15–30): Tech Stack Transparency Doc

This is the thing that builds trust with the analytically-minded decision makers — the managing partners, the firm principals who actually evaluate vendors.

Already drafted and saved at: `Assets/Credibility/tech-stack-transparency.md`

## May — Aim at the CPA Niche

Now you have certs, partner badges, a case study, and a tech stack doc. Time to point all of it at CPAs specifically.

### Week 1–2 (May 1–14): CPA Shadow Work Content Piece

This is your entry-point content. Something that speaks directly to the specific repetitive work that small CPA firms do and hate: document collection eating 5 hours a week, reconciliations done manually, deadline tracking on calendar alerts.

Already drafted and saved at: `Assets/Credibility/cpa-shadow-work-guide.md`

**How to use it:**
- Put it on your website as a guide or resource page.
- Share it on LinkedIn with a post that says something like: "Every CPA firm I talk to has the same 3 problems. Here's what they actually are — and what's realistic to fix."
- Use it as a leave-behind after any conversation with a CPA or someone who knows CPAs.

### Week 3–4 (May 15–31): Activate the Referral Path

This is where the Refined Wealth relationship becomes a business development asset.

- Your wealth management client almost certainly has a CPA they work with. Ask for an introduction. Not "do you know any CPAs who might need automation" — that's too vague. Ask: "I'm working with a few accounting firms on workflow automation. If you ever hear one of your CPA contacts complain about document collection or deadline tracking eating their time, I'd love an intro."
- Start showing up in CPA-relevant spaces on LinkedIn. Not selling. Just being visible as someone who understands their workflow problems. Comment on posts from CPA firms. Share the shadow work guide.

## What to Skip Entirely

- **AIT Designation (The Institutes)** — insurance-specific, not relevant to CPA niche
- **PMP** — experience requirements too high, overkill for solo boutique
- **CSCP** — designed for compliance practitioners at financial firms, not tech consultants
- **FINRA Series 7/SIE** — only needed if you're directly soliciting securities
- **Harvard/MIT/Stanford AI certificates** — wrong audience ($1,600–$23,000 range, designed for career switchers entering ML)
- **AWS AI Practitioner** — do after everything else; lower incremental value for CPA clients who are mostly on Microsoft
