# AI Discovery Prompts - Pre-Meeting Research

## Purpose
Templates for deploying AI agents to research prospects before sales conversations. These prompts gather maximum intelligence while identifying gaps that require human conversation.

---

## Research Framework

### Phase 1: Company & Industry Analysis
### Phase 2: Pain Point Hypothesis Development
### Phase 3: Technology Stack & Process Analysis
### Phase 4: Decision-Maker & Stakeholder Research
### Phase 5: Budget & Buying Signals
### Phase 6: Gap Identification & Question Prioritization

---

## Phase 1: Company & Industry Analysis

### Prompt Template

```
Research the following company for a potential AI automation engagement:

Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY if known, otherwise "unknown - please identify"]
Website: [URL if available]

Please provide:

1. COMPANY OVERVIEW
   - Core business model and services
   - Target customers and market position
   - Company size (employees, revenue if public)
   - Key products or service lines
   - Recent news, growth signals, or challenges

2. INDUSTRY CONTEXT
   - Industry trends affecting their business
   - Common pain points in this industry
   - Regulatory or compliance considerations
   - Competitive landscape and pressures
   - Technology adoption patterns in this sector

3. BUSINESS MODEL ANALYSIS
   - Revenue model (subscription, transaction, project-based)
   - Client acquisition process
   - Delivery/fulfillment model
   - Key operational workflows
   - Scaling challenges typical for this model

Format the output as a structured summary with specific facts and sources cited.
```

### Output Example
- Company overview with key facts
- Industry pain points relevant to AI automation
- Regulatory considerations (financial services compliance, healthcare privacy, legal ethics)
- Competitive pressures driving need for efficiency

## Phase 2: Pain Point Hypothesis Development

### Prompt Template

```
Based on the company analysis for [COMPANY NAME] in the [INDUSTRY] industry, develop hypotheses about their likely pain points and automation opportunities.

Focus on:

1. MANUAL PROCESS IDENTIFICATION
   - What processes are likely manual and time-consuming in this industry?
   - Which roles spend significant time on repetitive tasks?
   - What are typical workflow bottlenecks?

2. SCALING CHALLENGES
   - What prevents companies like this from growing without proportional headcount?
   - Where do quality or consistency issues emerge as they scale?
   - What tasks create capacity constraints?

3. COMPETITIVE PRESSURE
   - What are competitors doing with AI/automation?
   - Where is the industry moving that creates urgency?
   - What client expectations are rising that require new capabilities?

4. COST & EFFICIENCY OPPORTUNITIES
   - Where are they likely spending money on manual labor?
   - What tasks have high error rates or rework?
   - Which processes have long cycle times?

5. BRIDGE AI SOLUTIONS ALIGNMENT
   Based on the pain points identified, which Bridge AI Solutions services are most relevant:
   - LinkedIn Lead Generation & Automation
   - Contract Intelligence Systems
   - Landing Pages & Web Presence
   - Custom AI Strategy & Automation

For each relevant service, explain the specific pain point it would address.

Provide your analysis as prioritized hypotheses (most likely → least likely).
```

### Output Example
- Top 3-5 hypothesized pain points with reasoning
- Bridge AI service alignment recommendations
- Industry-specific automation opportunities
- Quantification estimates (e.g., "likely spending 15-20 hrs/week on manual prospecting")

## Phase 3: Technology Stack & Process Analysis

### Prompt Template

```
Research [COMPANY NAME]'s technology stack and operational processes:

1. CURRENT TECHNOLOGY STACK
   - CRM system (Salesforce, HubSpot, none, etc.)
   - Marketing automation tools
   - Communication platforms (email, Slack, etc.)
   - Industry-specific software (legal practice management, financial planning tools, etc.)
   - Website platform and tech stack
   - Known integrations or APIs

2. PROCESS MATURITY ASSESSMENT
   - Evidence of process documentation or standardization
   - Team size and structure (based on LinkedIn, job postings, etc.)
   - Technology adoption sophistication (early adopter vs. laggard)
   - Previous automation or AI initiatives (if mentioned publicly)

3. INTEGRATION OPPORTUNITIES
   - Which existing tools could AI agents integrate with?
   - What gaps exist in their current stack?
   - Where are manual handoffs between systems?

4. TECHNICAL READINESS
   - Comfort level with technology (based on website, content, job postings)
   - Data availability and quality indicators
   - API access to needed platforms
   - Compliance or security requirements that affect implementation

Cite specific sources (job postings, tech stack analysis sites, company blog posts, etc.).
```

### Output Example
- Tech stack inventory
- Process maturity assessment (low/medium/high)
- Integration opportunities for AI agents
- Technical barriers or requirements to note

## Phase 4: Decision-Maker & Stakeholder Research

### Prompt Template

```
Research the key decision-makers and stakeholders at [COMPANY NAME]:

1. PRIMARY CONTACT/LEAD
   Name: [NAME if known]
   - Role and responsibilities
   - Background and experience
   - LinkedIn profile insights (career path, interests, content they share)
   - Pain points they've mentioned publicly (interviews, posts, articles)
   - Communication style and preferences

2. ADDITIONAL STAKEHOLDERS
   Identify likely stakeholders in an AI automation decision:
   - C-suite executives (CEO, COO, CFO)
   - Technology decision-makers (CTO, IT Director)
   - Operations leaders
   - Department heads who would use the solution

   For each, note:
   - Current role
   - Likely priorities and concerns
   - Influence in buying decision

3. BUYING PROCESS INDICATORS
   - Company size → likely decision complexity
   - Industry → typical procurement requirements
   - Previous vendor relationships (technology partners mentioned on website)
   - Budget authority signals

4. RELATIONSHIP BUILDING OPPORTUNITIES
   - Shared connections (LinkedIn)
   - Common interests or backgrounds
   - Recent achievements or milestones to acknowledge
   - Content or causes they care about

Format as stakeholder map with decision influence assessment.
```

### Output Example
- Primary contact profile with rapport-building insights
- Stakeholder map (decision-maker, influencers, end-users)
- Buying process complexity assessment
- Relationship entry points

## Phase 5: Budget & Buying Signals

### Prompt Template

```
Analyze budget and buying signals for [COMPANY NAME]:

1. FINANCIAL INDICATORS
   - Company revenue (if public or estimated)
   - Funding status (bootstrapped, VC-backed, public)
   - Recent funding rounds or financial news
   - Budget allocation patterns in this industry/size

2. BUYING SIGNALS
   - Job postings related to operations, efficiency, or technology
   - Recent hires in relevant departments
   - Technology investments mentioned in news or blog
   - Growth initiatives or expansion plans
   - Pain points mentioned in public content

3. URGENCY INDICATORS
   - Upcoming events (conferences, product launches, busy season)
   - Competitive pressures mentioned
   - Regulatory changes affecting their business
   - Growth challenges or capacity constraints cited

4. BUDGET RANGE ESTIMATION
   Based on company size, industry, and pain points, estimate:
   - Likely budget range for AI automation project
   - ROI expectations (what multiple of investment do they likely expect?)
   - Payment preferences (monthly retainer vs. project-based)
   - Pilot vs. full implementation preference

Provide evidence-based estimates with reasoning.
```

### Output Example
- Financial health assessment
- Active buying signals identified
- Urgency drivers and timeline indicators
- Estimated budget range with justification

## Phase 6: Gap Identification & Question Prioritization

### Prompt Template

```
Based on all research conducted for [COMPANY NAME], create a gap analysis and prioritized question list:

1. KNOWLEDGE GAPS
   What critical information could NOT be found through research:
   - Current processes and workflows
   - Specific pain point quantification (hours, costs, impact)
   - Decision timeline and budget
   - Technical environment details
   - Stakeholder priorities and concerns
   - Previous automation attempts or challenges

2. VALIDATION NEEDS
   What hypotheses need human confirmation:
   - Assumed pain points
   - Technology stack details
   - Process maturity level
   - Decision-maker priorities

3. PRIORITIZED QUESTION LIST
   Create 5-7 strategic questions for the meeting, ordered by priority:

   Priority 1 (Must Ask):
   - [Question addressing biggest knowledge gap]

   Priority 2 (Should Ask):
   - [Questions validating key hypotheses]

   Priority 3 (Nice to Have):
   - [Questions for additional context]

4. CONVERSATION STRATEGY
   - Recommended opening approach
   - Key points to confirm early
   - Sensitive topics to address carefully
   - Closing questions to gauge fit and next steps

Format as a meeting preparation brief.
```

### Output Example
- Comprehensive gap list
- 5-7 prioritized strategic questions
- Conversation flow recommendations
- Red flags to watch for

## Usage Tips

1. **Run all phases sequentially** - Each builds on the previous
2. **Cite sources** - Note where information came from for credibility assessment
3. **Be specific** - Avoid generic industry advice; find company-specific insights
4. **Identify gaps explicitly** - What you don't know is as important as what you do
5. **Quantify when possible** - Hours, dollars, headcount, etc.
6. **Note confidence levels** - High confidence facts vs. reasonable assumptions vs. speculation
7. **Flag contradictions** - If research shows conflicting signals, note them
8. **Update after meeting** - Use AI to summarize meeting notes and update the profile
