# Follow-Up Templates - Email Sequences & Closing Workflows

## Purpose
Email templates and follow-up sequences for moving prospects from discovery through close. Customize these templates with client-specific details - never send generic.

---

## Follow-Up Sequence Overview

| Stage | Timing | Goal | Template |
|-------|--------|------|----------|
| Post-Meeting Summary | Same day | Confirm understanding, set expectations | Template 1 |
| Proposal Delivery | 2-5 days after meeting | Present proposal with context | Template 2 |
| Initial Check-In | 2-3 days after proposal | Answer questions, gauge interest | Template 3 |
| Value Reinforcement | 5-7 days after proposal | Share case study or insight | Template 4 |
| Final Check-In | 10-14 days after proposal | Create urgency, move to decision | Template 5 |
| Re-Engagement (Long-Term) | 30-90 days | Revive cold opportunities | Template 6 |

---

## Template 1: Post-Meeting Summary

### Purpose
Sent same day or within 24 hours of discovery meeting. Confirms understanding, builds trust, sets expectations for proposal.

### When to Use
- After every discovery or confirmation meeting
- Before sending formal proposal
- To ensure alignment and correct any misunderstandings

### Template

**Subject:** Great talking with you today - [Company Name] AI automation next steps

---

Hi [First Name],

Thanks for taking the time to meet today. I really enjoyed learning about [Company Name] and understanding how [specific pain point discussed] is affecting your [business/team/workflow].

Just to make sure I captured everything correctly, here's what I heard:

**Current Challenges:**
- [Pain point 1 with quantification - e.g., "Spending 15 hours/week on manual prospecting"]
- [Pain point 2 with quantification - e.g., "Following up with ~50 prospects manually in Excel"]
- [Pain point 3 - e.g., "Concerned about prospect leakage due to inconsistent tracking"]

**What Success Looks Like:**
- [Goal 1 - e.g., "Reclaim 10-12 hours per week for high-value client work"]
- [Goal 2 - e.g., "Increase pipeline by 2-3 qualified prospects per month"]
- [Goal 3 - e.g., "Maintain full control and compliance while automating research"]

**Timeline:**
- [Client's stated timeline - e.g., "Looking to have something in place by end of Q1"]

Did I get that right, or is there anything I missed or should adjust?

I'll put together a detailed proposal showing:
1. Exactly how we'd address each of these challenges
2. What the system would look like for your specific situation
3. Implementation timeline and pricing
4. ROI analysis based on the numbers you shared

I'll have this to you by [specific day - e.g., "end of day Thursday"]. Does [day/time - e.g., "Friday at 10am"] work for a 30-minute call to review it together?

## General Follow-Up Best Practices

### Timing
- **Same day:** Post-meeting summary
- **2-5 days:** Proposal delivery (don't rush, but don't drag)
- **2-3 days after proposal:** Initial check-in
- **5-7 days after proposal:** Value reinforcement
- **10-14 days after proposal:** Final check-in or create urgency
- **30-90 days:** Re-engagement for cold opportunities

### Tone
- Professional but conversational
- Confident, not desperate
- Helpful, not pushy
- Brief and scannable
- Personalized, never templated

### Subject Lines
- Reference their company name when possible
- Be specific, not vague ("Proposal" vs. "Following up")
- Create curiosity without being clickbaity
- Use "Re:" when continuing conversation thread

### Content
- Keep emails to 150 words or less when possible
- Use bold for key points
- Include specific next steps or calls to action
- Make it easy to respond (ask questions, offer options)
- Always provide value, never just "checking in"

### Follow-Up Persistence
- **3-5 touchpoints** is appropriate for serious opportunities
- **Space them out** - Don't spam
- **Vary the approach** - Check-in, value-add, urgency, exit
- **Know when to quit** - If no response after 5 touches, move on gracefully
- **Always leave door open** for future re-engagement
