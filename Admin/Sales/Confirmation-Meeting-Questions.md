# Confirmation Meeting Questions - Sales Conversation Guide

## Purpose
Question bank organized by conversation stage for confirming AI research findings, uncovering pain points, and qualifying opportunities. Use these as a guide, not a script - adapt to natural conversation flow.

---

## Meeting Structure Overview

| Stage | Time | Goal | Question Type |
|-------|------|------|---------------|
| Opening | 5 min | Build rapport, set agenda | Opening Questions |
| Confirmation | 10 min | Verify AI findings | Confirmation Questions |
| Gap-Filling | 10 min | Cover research blind spots | Gap-Filling Questions |
| Problem Depth | 15 min | Quantify pain & impact | Problem Depth Questions |
| Vision | 5 min | Explore desired state | Vision Questions |
| Decision Process | 5 min | Understand buying process | Decision Questions |
| Next Steps | 5 min | Gauge commitment | Next Steps Questions |

**Total: 55 minutes** (leave 5 min buffer for 60-min call)

---

## 1. Opening Questions (Build Rapport & Set Agenda)

### Goals
- Establish comfortable conversation dynamic
- Set expectations for the call
- Understand their objectives for the meeting
- Begin with their agenda, not yours

### Question Framework

**Opening Rapport Builder**
- "Thanks for making time today. Before we dive in, I'm curious - what prompted you to take this call?"
- "How's [recent achievement/news you found in research] going?"
- "I saw you've been at [Company] for [X years] - what's kept you there?"

**Agenda Setting**
- "I've done some research on [Company] and [Industry], but I'd love to hear directly from you. What's most important for you to get out of our conversation today?"
- "I typically like to spend most of our time understanding your current situation and challenges. Does that work for you?"
- "Do we have the full [30/60] minutes, or should I be mindful of a hard stop?"

**Their Objectives**
- "What would make this call a success for you?"
- "Is there anything specific you're hoping to learn or explore today?"

### Conversation Transition
"Great. Let me start by sharing what I understand about your business, and you can correct me where I'm off base..."

## 2. Confirmation Questions (Verify AI Research)

### Goals
- Demonstrate you've done homework
- Verify or correct research findings
- Build credibility through preparation
- Surface initial reactions to your understanding

### Question Framework

**Company & Business Model Confirmation**
- "My understanding is that [Company] primarily focuses on [service/product]. Is that accurate, or is there more to it?"
- "It looks like your main clients are [customer type]. Is that your core market, or are you expanding?"
- "From what I can see, you're working with [team size/structure]. Has that changed recently?"

**Process & Workflow Confirmation**
- "I imagine a typical [workflow] for you looks something like [describe process]. Does that match your experience?"
- "It seems like [role/team] probably handles [task]. Is that right?"
- "Based on your industry, I'd guess you're using tools like [CRM/software]. What's your current tech stack?"

**Industry & Competitive Confirmation**
- "I know [industry trend/regulation] has been affecting firms like yours. How has that impacted your business?"
- "Are you seeing competitors adopt [technology/approach] in your space?"

**Pain Point Confirmation (Soft Entry)**
- "One pattern I've noticed in [industry] is [common pain point]. Is that something you're experiencing?"
- "I'd imagine [workflow] takes a fair amount of time. Is that a challenge for your team?"

### Listen For
- Where they correct you (signals priorities)
- Where they expand enthusiastically (signals pain or opportunity)
- Where they're vague or defensive (signals sensitivity or uncertainty)
- Quantifications they volunteer ("we spend 20 hours a week on...")

## 3. Gap-Filling Questions (Cover Research Blind Spots)

### Goals
- Gather information AI couldn't find
- Understand internal processes and culture
- Assess technical environment and readiness
- Identify stakeholders and decision dynamics

### Question Framework

**Current State Deep Dive**
- "Walk me through what a typical [day/week/month] looks like for [you/your team]."
- "What does your current process for [key workflow] look like from start to finish?"
- "How are you currently handling [specific task related to pain point]?"
- "When did you start noticing [problem] becoming an issue?"

**Resource & Capacity**
- "How much of your team's time currently goes to [manual task]?"
- "What would you do with an extra [X hours] per week if you had it?"
- "Have you tried to address this by hiring? What happened?"

**Technology & Tools**
- "What tools are working well for you? What's frustrating?"
- "Have you looked at [relevant technology] before? What stopped you from implementing it?"
- "How open is your team to adopting new technologies?"
- "Any IT or security requirements I should know about?"

**Previous Attempts**
- "Have you tried to solve this problem before? What happened?"
- "What's prevented you from automating [process] until now?"
- "Is there anything about past vendor experiences that I should know?"

**Team & Stakeholders**
- "Who else would be involved in evaluating or using a solution like this?"
- "How does your team typically react to process changes?"
- "Is there anyone I should loop into this conversation?"

## 4. Problem Depth Questions (Quantify Impact)

### Goals
- Quantify pain in time, money, and opportunity cost
- Uncover emotional drivers (frustration, fear, aspiration)
- Establish urgency and priority
- Build case for ROI

### Question Framework Structure
Use the "Problem → Impact → Cost" sequence for each pain point:

#### A) SITUATION QUESTIONS (Current State)
- "How are you currently handling [process/task]?"
- "How long has this been a challenge?"
- "How often does [problem] occur?"

#### B) PROBLEM AWARENESS QUESTIONS (Uncover Pain)
- "What's difficult about the current approach?"
- "What breaks down most often in this process?"
- "What keeps you up at night about [area]?"
- "What's the worst-case scenario if this continues?"

#### C) IMPACT QUESTIONS (Quantify Consequences)

**Time Impact:**
- "How much time does [task] take each week/month?"
- "What could you accomplish if you had that time back?"
- "How much time do you spend fixing errors or redoing work?"

**Financial Impact:**
- "What's the cost of [problem] in terms of lost revenue/opportunity?"
- "How much are you spending on manual labor for [task]?"
- "What does it cost when [error/delay] happens?"

**Opportunity Cost:**
- "What strategic initiatives are getting delayed because of this?"
- "What could you pursue if you had more capacity?"
- "How is this affecting your ability to grow?"

**Quality & Risk Impact:**
- "How often do errors occur with the manual process?"
- "What's at risk if [problem] isn't addressed?"
- "How does this affect client experience or satisfaction?"

**Emotional/Cultural Impact:**
- "How does your team feel about spending time on [manual task]?"
- "What would it mean for you personally if this was solved?"
- "How is this affecting morale or retention?"

#### D) IMPLICATION QUESTIONS (Compound the Pain)
- "If this continues for another [6 months/year], what happens?"
- "How does this problem affect other areas of your business?"
- "What else would improve if you solved this?"

### Specific Question Examples by Service Area

**For LinkedIn Lead Generation:**
- "How many hours per week do you spend on prospecting and outreach?"
- "What's your current conversion rate from connection to meeting?"
- "How many meaningful conversations could you have if you weren't doing manual research?"
- "What's the lifetime value of a new client in your practice?"
- "How many potential clients are you not reaching because of capacity constraints?"

**For Contract Intelligence:**
- "How long does a typical contract review take?"
- "How many contracts do you review per month?"
- "What's your billing rate for contract review time?"
- "How often do you find issues that could have been caught earlier?"
- "What matters are you turning away because you don't have capacity?"

**For Web Presence/Landing Pages:**
- "How many potential clients visit your website per month?"
- "What percentage actually reach out?"
- "How many opportunities are you losing because of first-impression concerns?"
- "What does a new client mean for your business financially?"

**For Custom Automation:**
- "Walk me through the most time-consuming process in your business."
- "How many people touch this process?"
- "What's the total labor cost of this workflow per month?"
- "How many errors or delays happen with the current manual process?"

### Listen For (Critical)
- **Specific numbers** - Hours, dollars, frequency, volume
- **Emotional language** - "Frustrating," "overwhelming," "keeping me up at night"
- **Urgency signals** - "We can't keep doing this," "something has to change"
- **Personal impact** - "I'm spending weekends on this," "I'm missing time with family"
- **Strategic constraints** - "We can't grow until we solve this"

### If They're Vague
"Help me understand the magnitude... if you had to estimate..."
- "How many hours per week would you say?"
- "If you had to put a dollar value on it, what would you estimate?"
- "In terms of priority, where does this rank for you - top 3? Top 5?"

## 5. Vision Questions (Explore Desired Future State)

### Goals
- Paint picture of solution value
- Assess alignment with Bridge AI capabilities
- Understand success criteria
- Gauge openness to AI solutions

### Question Framework

**Ideal State**
- "If we could wave a magic wand and solve this perfectly, what would change?"
- "What would success look like 6 months from now?"
- "How would your day-to-day be different if this was handled?"
- "What would you do with the time/capacity you'd gain?"

**Value Prioritization**
- "What's more important - time savings, revenue growth, or risk reduction?"
- "If you could only solve one aspect of this problem, what would it be?"
- "What would make the biggest difference for your business?"

**Solution Openness**
- "Have you thought about using AI to address this?"
- "What's your comfort level with AI-assisted workflows?"
- "How important is it that you maintain control vs. full automation?"
- "Would you prefer a solution that handles everything, or one where you review key decisions?"

**Success Metrics**
- "How would you measure whether a solution was working?"
- "What would need to happen for this to be a home run?"
- "What would justify the investment for you?"

**Constraints & Requirements**
- "What constraints should I be aware of (compliance, security, team preferences)?"
- "What would be non-negotiable in a solution?"
- "What failed approaches should we avoid?"

## 6. Decision Process Questions (Understand Buying Journey)

### Goals
- Identify decision-makers and influencers
- Understand timeline and urgency
- Uncover budget range and authority
- Map out path to close

### Question Framework

**Timeline & Urgency**
- "What's driving the timing on this? Why now vs. six months ago?"
- "Is there a deadline or event creating urgency?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this in the next [timeframe]?"
- "How quickly would you want to get started if we're a fit?"

**Decision-Making Process**
- "Walk me through how you typically evaluate and select solutions like this."
- "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
- "Are you the final decision-maker, or do others need to sign off?"
- "What criteria will you use to evaluate options?"

**Budget & Investment**
- "Have you set aside budget for this, or is this exploratory?"
- "How do you typically think about investments like this - ROI multiple, payback period?"
- "What's your expectation for how quickly this should pay for itself?"
- "Do you prefer project-based pricing or ongoing retainer relationships?"

**Stakeholder Alignment**
- "How does your team feel about addressing this problem?"
- "Is everyone aligned that this is a priority, or will you need to build internal support?"
- "Who would be the day-to-day user of a solution?"

**Alternatives & Competition**
- "What other approaches are you considering?"
- "Are you talking to other vendors or solutions?"
- "What would you do if we weren't in the picture?"

**Risk Tolerance**
- "How do you typically mitigate risk with new vendors?"
- "Would you prefer to start with a pilot or dive into a full implementation?"
- "What would need to be true for you to feel confident moving forward?"

### Listen For
- **Budget authority** - Can they approve, or do they need permission?
- **Timeline signals** - Real urgency vs. "someday"
- **Competitive intel** - Who else they're considering
- **Red flags** - Unrealistic expectations, process paralysis, unclear authority

### If Budget is Unclear
"I want to make sure I'm proposing something that makes sense for you. Just so I have a sense of scale, are we talking about an investment you'd expect to be in the thousands, tens of thousands, or higher range?"

## 7. Next Steps Questions (Gauge Commitment & Close)

### Goals
- Assess genuine interest vs. politeness
- Propose clear next actions
- Get commitment to timeline
- Set expectations for follow-up

### Question Framework

**Interest Assessment**
- "Based on what we've discussed, does this feel like something worth exploring further?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how much of a priority is solving this problem right now?"
- "What questions or concerns do you have at this point?"

**Proposal Positioning**
- "What I'd like to do is put together a specific proposal showing how we'd address [pain points discussed] and what the ROI would look like for you. Would that be helpful?"
- "I typically include a couple of different options - does that make sense for you?"
- "What information would you need in a proposal to make a decision?"

**Timeline Commitment**
- "When would be the best time to review a proposal together?"
- "How much time do you need to review before we reconnect?"
- "Who else should I include on the proposal?"

**Blocking & Tackling**
- "Is there anything that would prevent you from moving forward if the proposal makes sense financially?"
- "What concerns should I make sure to address in the proposal?"
- "Are there any showstoppers I should know about now?"

**Clear Next Action**
- "Here's what I'm thinking for next steps: [outline]. Does that work for you?"
- "I'll get you a proposal by [date]. Can we schedule 30 minutes on [date] to review it together?"
- "Between now and then, is there anything else you need from me?"

**Relationship Building**
- "Is there anyone else in your network who might be facing similar challenges?"
- "I'd love to stay connected even if timing isn't right - are you on LinkedIn?"

### Strong Close Language
- "I'm confident we can help you [achieve outcome]. Let me show you exactly how that would work and what it would cost."
- "Based on what you've shared, I think we could get you from [current state] to [desired state] within [timeline]. Let me map that out in detail."
- "I think there's a strong fit here. Let's get specific about what this would look like for [Company]."

### Soft Close Language (if less qualified)
- "This sounds like it could be a fit. Let me put together some thoughts and we can explore whether it makes sense."
- "I appreciate you sharing all this. Let me think about the best approach and I'll follow up with some ideas."

## Question Selection Strategy

### Before the Meeting
1. Review AI Discovery Summary
2. Identify top 3 hypothesized pain points
3. Select 5-7 priority questions that fill gaps
4. Prepare follow-up questions for likely answers
5. Print or have this guide open (but don't read from it)

### During the Meeting
- **Start broad, then narrow** - Situation → Problem → Impact → Implication
- **Follow their energy** - When they get animated, go deeper
- **Use silence** - Count to 5 after they finish; they'll often add the most valuable info
- **Paraphrase for clarity** - "So if I'm hearing you right, you're spending 15 hours a week on this?"
- **Ask "tell me more"** - Three most powerful words in sales
- **Watch for time** - Cover depth questions before you hit 30 minutes

### What NOT to Do
- Don't pitch features - Ask about problems
- Don't jump to solutions - Stay in discovery mode
- Don't interrogate - Make it a conversation
- Don't multi-task - Give full attention
- Don't assume - Verify everything
- Don't talk more than they do - 70/30 rule (they talk 70%)

## Post-Meeting Action Items

Immediately after the call:
1. **Document key quotes** - Especially quantifications and emotional language
2. **Update AI Discovery Summary** - Correct assumptions, fill gaps
3. **Identify solution fit** - Which Bridge AI services match their needs
4. **Calculate preliminary ROI** - Use their numbers
5. **Draft follow-up email** - Summarize key points and confirm next steps
6. **Add to CRM/tracking** - Move to appropriate pipeline stage
7. **Block time for proposal** - Schedule proposal development work

## Red Flags That Indicate Poor Fit

Watch for these signals during the conversation:

- **Vague on pain points** - Can't quantify impact or urgency
- **No budget discussion** - Unwilling to even discuss investment range
- **Unrealistic expectations** - Wants 100% automation with zero oversight
- **Price shopping** - Only focused on cost, not value
- **No authority** - Can't influence decision, just gathering info
- **No timeline** - "Someday" or "just exploring"
- **Technology resistant** - Skeptical of AI, won't adopt new tools
- **Previous vendor bad experiences** - Blames everyone else, no ownership

If you see 3+ red flags, consider whether to invest time in proposal.

---

## Green Flags That Indicate Strong Fit

- **Specific quantification** - "We spend 20 hours a week on this"
- **Emotional language** - "This is killing us," "I'm drowning"
- **Clear timeline** - "We need to solve this by Q2"
- **Budget awareness** - Discusses ROI expectations, payback period
- **Decision authority** - "I can approve this" or "I'll need to run it by [specific person]"
- **Strategic importance** - "This is blocking our growth"
- **Technology openness** - "We're ready to embrace AI"
- **Collaborative tone** - "How would you approach this?"

If you see 4+ green flags, prioritize this opportunity.
