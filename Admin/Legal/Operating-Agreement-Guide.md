## Utah-Specific Requirements

### Is an Operating Agreement Required in Utah?
**No, but HIGHLY RECOMMENDED.**

According to Utah law (UT Code § 48-3a-112), Utah does not require LLCs to have a written operating agreement, but the government strongly encourages it. Without one, your LLC will be governed by Utah's default LLC rules.

**Source:** [Utah LLC Operating Agreement Requirements](https://www.bizreport.com/operating-agreement-utah)

### Why You Need One in Utah:
1. **Asset Protection:** Reinforces separation between personal and business finances, protecting your limited liability status
2. **Dispute Resolution:** Without an agreement, disputes default to Utah Code § 48-3a rules
3. **Banking:** Most banks require an operating agreement as proof of LLC ownership
4. **Credibility:** Professional appearance to investors, partners, and clients

**Source:** [Utah LLC Laws](https://howtostartanllc.com/llc-statute/utah)

### Filing Requirements:
✅ **You do NOT need to file your Operating Agreement with:**
- Division of Corporations and Commercial Code
- Utah Department of Commerce
- Your Registered Agent
- IRS or any government agency

**It's an "internal document"** - just keep a copy with your business records.

**Source:** [Northwest Registered Agent - Utah Operating Agreement](https://www.northwestregisteredagent.com/llc/utah/operating-agreement)
